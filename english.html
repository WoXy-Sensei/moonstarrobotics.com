About Us

Who Are We? What Are Our Goals?

We are team number 7444 of the First Foundation's FRC team. Our Moon Star Robotics Team was established in September 2018 with the partnership of our school's Electrical-Electronics, Machinery, and Motor Vehicles departments with 10 students and 4 mentors. Our team, which has made a name for itself in a short time, has increased to 50 students as of 2024. Our team, established at Borusan Asım Kocabıyık Vocational Technical and Anatolian High School, continues its initiatives with the slogan "It won't end until you win." The championship we won in 2023 is one of our greatest motivations. Continuing on the path of this championship, our primary duty is to add experience and fun time to our team day by day.

What is Tulpar?

Tulpar is a legendary creature with an important place in Turkish mythology. Depicted as a winged horse, Tulpar is a magnificent and powerful figure. However, Tulpar's wings are invisible to everyone; only heroes and noble-spirited people can see these wings. This special ability is an important element that enhances Tulpar's mystery and power. Tulpar symbolizes hope and courage, so it inspires people in difficult times and encourages them to overcome challenges. Tulpar's role in mythology goes beyond being just a physical entity. It is the most loyal companion of warriors and heroes, providing them with speed and endurance. In Turkish epics, heroes on Tulpar often achieve great victories and bring peace to their people. Therefore, Tulpar is not just a mythical creature but also a symbol that holds a place in the collective memory of a nation. Tulpar's legend points to the rich and deep roots of Turkish culture. This legendary horse not only physically transports people but also elevates their spirits. Tulpar's wings are like a door that always opens to the sky; it is a symbol of hope, freedom, and greatness. Additionally, until 2024, our robot's name has remained "Tulpar."

Awards such as "Engineering Inspiration" and "Impact" given for FRC address the issues of sharing and spreading FRC and its contributions with others. Additionally, you can see the financial aid that FRC teams provide to different schools or associations.
